The objective of a hackathon is to encourage innovation, collaboration, and prototyping among participants, providing a hands-on learning experience and opportunities for networking. Hackathons aim to bring together individuals from diverse backgrounds and skill levels to develop creative solutions to real-world problems, fostering a dynamic and engaging environment that promotes creativity, experimentation, and fun. By participating in a hackathon, individuals can gain new skills and knowledge in a specific domain or technology, build functional prototypes or minimum viable products, and connect with like-minded individuals, industry experts, and potential investors or partners. Hackathons also focus on solving specific problems or challenges, often with a emphasis on social impact or industry-specific issues, driving innovation and progress in various fields.
